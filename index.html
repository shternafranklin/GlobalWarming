The GreenHouse Effect

The Greenhouse gases, keep the earth warm through the process called the Greenhouse Effect. The Earth gets energy from the sun in the form of sunlight. The Earth's surface absorbs some of this energy and heats up. That's why the surface of a road can feel hot even after the sun sets. The Earth cools down by giving off a different form of energy, called infrared radiation. But before all this radiation can escape to outer space, greenhouse gases in the atmosphere absorb some of it, which makes the atmosphere warmer. As the atmosphere gets warmer, it makes the Earth's surface warmer, too.

So whats the problem?

The increase of burning fossil fuels such as coals, oil and natural gas, produce carbon dioxide, which traps heat in the atmosphere, which makes the Earth warmer. The increase in the concentration of greenhouse gases, contribute to the magnitude of the greenhouse effect, which results in global warming. Humans are adding carbon to the atmosphere faster than natural processes can remove it, thereby contributing to the global climate change.




IMPACTS


How will climate change affect you? Your community? The environment around you?

  • Agriculture

    The crops that we grow for food need specific conditions to thrive, including the right temperature and enough water. A changing climate could have both positive and negative effects on crops.

  • Energy

    Global climate change will affect how much and when we need energy. As temperatures rise, there will be in increase in air conditioning, which uses a lot of electricity.

  • Health
    If the planets temperature continues to rise, there will be an increase in heat waves, causing illnesses such as heat cramps, heat strokes and poor air quality. In warmer temperatures, ticks and mosquitoes can survive longer throughout the year and expand their ranges of endangering people.

  • Water Resources

    Warm waters impact many kinds of sea life, such as corals and fish.

  • Wildlife

    Our country is home to a diverse array of wildlife ranging from the highest peaks, to deserts, to freshwater and marine environments. Increasing global temperatures are expected to disrupt ecosystems, pushing to extinction those species that cannot adapt.

  • Forest
    Forests, which are sensitive to climates such as shifting weather patters, droughts, and wildfires, provide homes for many kinds of plants and animals, protect water quality, and provide people with wood.



  • Storm

    Due to high temperature, the oceans absorb more heat from the atmosphere and become warmer which increases the frequency of hurricanes and other tropical storms, heavier rainstorms, and snowstorms.

  • Glaciers

    Glaciers are large sheets of snow and ice, warmer temperature cause glaciers to melt faster than they can accumulate new snow. As glaciers melt they add more water into the ocean, which raises the sea level.

  • Rising Sea Level

    The rising sea level are due to the melting of moutain glaciers and the ocean temperature wreaking havoc to coastal structures and local sea life.
